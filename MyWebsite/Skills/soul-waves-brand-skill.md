# Soul Waves Brand System

This skill contains the complete brand system for Soul Waves, Anna Schuster's digital product builder business. Every output (UI, copy, artifacts, components, pages) must follow these rules.

## Quick Reference: Design Tokens

### Colors

| Token | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| `--sw-brown` | `#44140D` | Primary dark. Nav, footer, headings, body text |
| `--sw-cream` | `#FFF8EC` | Background. Page base, card backgrounds, light sections |
| `--sw-red` | `#8C1C13` | Deep accent. Links, decorative lines, logo |
| `--sw-copper` | `#C5783C` | Golden accent. CTAs, hover states, pricing highlights, key moments. THIS IS THE GOLDEN ELEMENT. Use it prominently. |
| `--sw-sand` | `#E6CCB2` | Warm neutral. Borders, dividers, subtle backgrounds, section alternation |

**Usage rules:**
- Background is always `--sw-cream` or white. Never dark mode unless specifically requested.
- CTAs (buttons, links that matter) use `--sw-copper` as background with `--sw-cream` text, or `--sw-copper` text on cream.
- Hover states shift toward `--sw-red` or darken `--sw-copper` slightly.
- Body text is `--sw-brown`. Never pure black (#000).
- Subtle gradients allowed: `--sw-cream` → white, or a warm glow using `--sw-copper` at low opacity behind premium sections.

### Typography

| Font | Role | Weight | Rules |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Great Vibes** | "Soul Waves" title, section headings | 400 | Decorative only. Max 3 words per use. The signature font. Use for section titles, the brand name, decorative headers. Never for body text or long phrases. |
| **Tsukimi Rounded** | Subheadings, body text, UI elements | 400 (body), 500 (subheadings) | The workhorse. NEVER bold. Never weight 600+. This is a hard rule. |
| **Courier Prime** | Frieda chatbot widget ONLY | 400 | Gives the chatbot its own personality. Never use outside the chatbot. |

**What was dropped:** Meow Script (was in old brand board). Removed. One script font is enough.

**Global no-bold rule:** Max font-weight 600 applies ONLY to Great Vibes headings. Tsukimi Rounded stays 400-500 everywhere. Nothing on the site should feel heavy or shouty.

**Never use em dashes anywhere.** Use commas, colons, periods, or restructure. This applies to all copy, UI text, descriptions, everywhere.

### Logo

Sun/waves motif inside a circle with "SOUL WAVES" text. The logo works in:
- Full color on cream backgrounds
- `--sw-red` or `--sw-brown` monochrome
- Never on dark backgrounds without testing contrast

### Imagery

- Ocean, water, tropical coastal settings
- Warm natural light (late afternoon sun on water is the golden vibe)
- Real photos of Anna working in cafés, swimming, traveling
- Never stock-photo-looking staged shots
- Never corporate office imagery

## Voice and Copy Rules

### Who Anna is (for copy context)

Austrian digital product builder. Background: prison guard, escape room manager, bakery, pedagogy studies. Currently based in Lombok, Indonesia as a digital nomad. Runs Soul Waves Solutions LLC (Wyoming). The contrast between the background and the current work IS the brand.

### One-liner

"I build and run the digital back-end of your business."

### What Soul Waves does

Websites, course platforms (Kajabi, Skool, WordPress, Momoyoga, ThriveCart), high-conversion Shopify stores, AI chatbots, automations, video editing, visual assets. Built properly, kept running, fixed when it breaks.

### What Soul Waves is NOT (important for copy)

- Not a copywriting service. Builds structure, not sentences. Clients provide their own text or use templates.
- Not a generic VA. No inbox, calendar, or travel management.
- Not an agency. One person, defined scope.
- Not a social media manager.

### Voice characteristics

- Stream of consciousness that loops back on itself (real, not messy)
- Self-aware humor, especially self-deprecating
- Honest about confusion and mess without performing vulnerability
- Short bursts when something lands: "Freedom tastes so good." "Worth it though."
- Warm, intelligent, grounded, slightly bold, a little unusual
- Spiritual and grounded at the same time: cacao ceremonies and WordPress bugs coexist

### Copy rules

- Never push. Invite. (Generator in Human Design: responds, doesn't initiate)
- Never fake urgency. No "limited spots." No countdown timers. Trust timing.
- The ✨ emoji is intentional and part of the personality. Never remove it.
- No em dashes. Ever.
- No filler words: game-changing, unlock, next level, level up
- No "It's not X, it's Y" framing
- No one-line drama stacking
- No AI-sounding cadence (smooth, symmetrical, predictably structured)
- No generic VA or coach tone
- No tutorial-account energy
- No overselling authority that isn't there yet
- No "drop a comment if you agree" endings
- No hashtag spam
- Post endings: short, dry, punchy, or nothing. "Worth it though." is the gold standard. The last strong line stands alone. Never add softening questions.

### Tone by context

**Website copy:** First person, natural speech rhythm. Lead with the unusual. Short sentences. Occasional long one for rhythm. Dry, grounded, slightly bold.

**LinkedIn:** Thinking out loud, building in public, honest observations. 150-250 words. English first.

**Outreach:** No fake small talk. Open honestly, get to the point. Show real understanding of their business. Direct, unusual, charming, lightly cheeky, intelligent, respectful.

**Client messages:** Warm but professional. Clear updates. Short paragraphs. Honest about delays.

## Website Architecture

### Click-to-Close Flow

Every page funnels to one of three actions:

**Primary CTA: Book a free discovery call**
- Link: calendar.app.google/WKhMs5XL1DeaccdK6
- Button style: `--sw-copper` background, `--sw-cream` text
- Appears on every page, visible without excessive scrolling

**Secondary CTA: Talk to Frieda**
- Floating chatbot widget, bottom-right
- Frame: "Not sure what you need? Ask Frieda."
- Frieda answers FAQs, qualifies leads, routes to booking

**Tertiary CTA: Email or contact form**
- anna@soulwavesva.com
- Simple form: name, business type, what they need, how they found Soul Waves

### Page structure

**Homepage:** Hero (one-liner + book a call button + Frieda floating) → what I do (short, visual) → who I help → testimonials → book a call again at bottom. Not an exhaustive service list. A warm, clear invitation.

**Services / What I Build:** Clear categories (websites, Shopify stores, course platforms, chatbots, retainers). Each ends with CTA. No public pricing except retainer range (€300-400/month) to filter.

**About:** The real story. Prison guard to digital nomad. Not a bio, a story. Ends with CTA.

**Contact:** Simple. Book a call (primary), email (secondary), social links (tertiary).

### 5-second test

A visitor who lands and scrolls for 5 seconds must understand:
1. Who: Anna, Austrian digital product builder, unusual background, real human
2. What: Builds and runs digital back-end (websites, platforms, stores, chatbots)
3. Why her: Handles the tech so you don't have to. One person, defined scope, no chaos. Not a copywriter, not an agency.
4. Next step: Talk to Frieda, book a call, or fill in the form

### What the website must NOT do

- Display a confusing grid of services with no hierarchy
- Use "Soul Waves VA" branding (it's "Soul Waves" or "Soul Waves Solutions")
- Show old package names (Namaste, Glow, Elevate)
- Lead with spiritual language that obscures what the business actually does
- Make visitors guess the next step
- Hide the booking link behind multiple pages
- Look like a template site any VA could have
- Use bold Tsukimi Rounded anywhere
